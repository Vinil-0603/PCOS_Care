About PCOS

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder common among women of reproductive age. It may cause infrequent or prolonged menstrual periods, excess androgen levels, and polycystic ovaries.

The exact cause of PCOS is unknown, but factors that might play a role include:

  • Excess insulin production
  • Low-grade inflammation
  • Heredity factors
  • Excess androgen production

PCOS can lead to various health issues, including infertility, metabolic problems, and mental health concerns. Early diagnosis and treatment can help reduce the risk of long-term complications.
